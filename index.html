Gender Based Violence

Girls coders in Lango Region against Gender Based Violence


Gender-based violence is violence directed against a person because of their gender. Both women and men experience gender-based violence but the majority of victims are women and girls.

Focus prevention efforts on changing social norms that underpin violence against children and GBV, support economic empowerment for women and adolescent girls, and preventing violence against children at school, including school clubs, gender-differentiated sanitary facilities, and peer-to-peer learning.

Types of Gender Based Violence include


Physical violence:

any act that causes physical harm to the victim through direct aggression.


Psychological violence:

any act that causes emotional or mental harm to the victim, such as threats, insults, humiliation, isolation, or manipulation.


Sexual violence:

any act that violates the sexual autonomy or integrity of the victim, such as rape, sexual assault, sexual harassment, or forced prostitution.


Economic violence:

any act that deprives the victim of their economic resources or opportunities, such as withholding money, controlling expenses, or preventing access to education or employment.
